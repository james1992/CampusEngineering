May 2018

NAVIGATOR SOFT-LAUNCH SITE IS LIVE

LABS.MAPS.UW.EDU

Business Applications is proud to announce the internal launch of our pedestrian navigation website: Navigator. This free, no-login, and mobile-friendly website is designed to make getting around on campus easier, with less time wasted wandering the halls. Navigator provides step by step directions to over 14,000 possible destinations, both in and outdoors. The site has routes for nearly every academic, housing, and administrative building on the Seattle campus.

YOUR FEEDBACK IS VITAL

Our soft-launch, which kicked off on May 21st, is focused on improving the user experience around the feedback we receive from staff and Students. How you move through the website, which features are less than obvious, and what strange terms are commonly searched for will all be looked at. To send us feedback, click the envelope icon on the sidebar, or contact the Customer Care Team with the subject “Navigator Feedback.”

July-August 2017

PDF Floor Plan Printing

MAPS is proud to present PDF floor plan printing for entire buildings or selected floors. Users can now print PDF floor plans that show specific GeoSIMS space information such as space by category, by department, or by primary use. The pre-defined layouts provide a color coded print that is automatically rotated to fit the page perfectly and give you the best display possible.

Printing a PDF Floor Plan

1) Click on the links icon at the bottom of the info page for a selected building or floor.

2) Select a PDF Floor Plan Print.

3) Give your print its own title.

4) Save them to your computer or print them straight from the browser!
  (Works best with Chrome - May have issues with Internet Explorer)

Our interactive maps not only let you travel the exteriors of campus, but they also let you dive inside buildings, floors, and rooms. They can even take you to distant lands like Friday Harbor or Big Beef Creek.

But wait...there’s more!

Come learn how our maps can help you keep your assets in check.

  • A Picture is Worth a Thousand Words: Our maps can provide a graphical representation of your asset data by linking it with an interactive map we continuously maintain.
  • One Big Mappy Family: Our maps can link your asset data with asset data from other departments.

MAPS has some new developments!

Environmental Health & Safety - Evacuation Map Editor

Evacuation Map Editor was developed for Environmental Health & Safety (EH&S) to track and manage evacuation maps, evacuation routes, emergency assembly points, and other related assets.

This initiative replaced inefficient and expensive practices that could no longer be funded.

We used to hire outside graphic designers to develop the artwork and we had no ability to make edits. We are now doing this in house at a much lower cost when there is a need using UW students. This initiative aligns with TAP principles. The current project to develop evacuation route maps for campus buildings has exceeded my expectations. EH&S is data rich and we are looking for opportunities to make better use of this information. I believe there may be many opportunities to consider. It has been a very good investment.
Mark Murray, Assistant Director, EH&S

For more information on building evacuation routes visit:
http://www.ehs.washington.edu/fsobuilding/buildevac.shtm

Treasury Office - UW Building Bond Viewer

The Treasury team, with help from Capital Planning & Development and bond counsel, just went live with a new website that will help departments track private use in tax-exempt buildings… When we issue tax-exempt bonds, we have to be able to assure that we are compliant with IRS regulations regarding use. This site helps to raise awareness of the issue, shares a guide to managing private use, and has a map showing which facilities on campus are financed with tax-exempt debt.
Chris Malins, Associate Vice President, Treasury Office

January - February 2017

MAPS Is Now Live!

MAPS GIS (Geographic Information System) is now available online!

Discover how MAPS Services can help with all your data management needs.

If you have the data... we can map it!

Our new robust GIS infrastructure can link information from database systems throughout the campus and create maps showing the space data.

For example, MAPS could create an interactive map showing the locations and contact info for members of your department. Other projects include mapping of locations and data for fire extinguishers, emergency evacuation routes, construction project boundaries, floor coverings, recycling and compost bins, or even the closest places to get coffee on the campus. MAPS GIS can be used to better understand the utilization of campus spaces and the resources required to maintain them. The possibilities are endless! You provide and maintain the data. We provide the maps and applications to manage it.
